In Old Shatterhand's quest to find Winnetou, who had traveled East on an immensely important mission, Old Shatterhand gets caught in a hurricane in the waters off Cuba. Stranded on an island, he eventually finds himself wandering aimlessly in New York City and ultimately applies to become a detective. While searching for William Ohlert, the mentally ill son of a banker, Old Shatterhand receives help from a famous tracker named Old Death, who is either an active or former narcotics addict. Although Old Shatterhand does encounter Winnetou, their meeting is bittersweet, as Winnetou cannot stop to greet him due to a secret mission entrusted to him by Benito Juarez, the Indian-descended president of Mexico. The two become entangled in conflicts involving members of the Ku Klux Klan, a direct consequence of the ongoing Civil War between the North and the South. Their journey extends not only to the Mexican border but also into the Mapimi desert in Mexico. Old Shatterhand is left astonished upon meeting Old Firehand, another renowned westman, as he uncovers a mysterious love triangle that also involves Winnetou. This book also features a poem written by someone suffering from mental illness, which turns out to be a reflection of Karl May's own life experiences. In this second part of the trilogy, Old Shatterhand concludes that behind every evil act committed by Native Americans, there is always a white man at its root! And what becomes of the narcotics addict? You'll have to read to find out!



Author:Karl May

CLIVE CUSSLER

Clive Cussler was a prolific American author and underwater explorer, best known for his adventure novels featuring the character Dirk Pitt1. Born on July 15, 1931, in Aurora, Illinois, Cussler’s career spanned several decades, during which he wrote over 80 books1. His works often topped The New York Times bestseller list1. Cussler’s novels are renowned for their thrilling plots and detailed descriptions of underwater exploration, reflecting his own passion for marine archaeology. He founded the National Underwater and Marine Agency (NUMA), which has discovered numerous shipwrecks1. Some of his popular series include the “Dirk Pitt” series, the “NUMA Files,” and the "Oregon Files"1
